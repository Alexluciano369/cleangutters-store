Headlines · Subject Lines · Ad Hooks · Booking Scripts · Review Asks · Follow-ups
How to use this: Don't copy these word-for-word — swap in your trade, your city, your numbers. The point is the structure. A headline that works for gutters works for HVAC, roofing, landscaping, or plumbing once you swap the specifics. Each "Why it works" tells you the psychology so you can write ten more of your own.

Section 1 — Headlines That Get Clicked

Use these for landing pages, Google Business posts, and ad headlines.

"Cherry Hill's Highest-Rated Gutter Cleaning — 847 Reviews, 4.9 Stars"
Use as: homepage / map-pack headline
Why it works: Specific location + specific proof (847, 4.9) signals credibility instantly. Numbers beat adjectives every time.
"Clogged Gutters? We'll Clear Them This Week — Starting at $149"
Use as: storm-season ad
Why it works: Names the exact pain, the speed of relief, and an honest price. Removes the #1 friction (uncertainty about cost).
"The Gutter Crew Your Neighbors Already Trust"
Use as: Nextdoor / local social
Why it works: Social proof + locality. "Your neighbors" makes it feel safe and pre-vetted.
"Don't Wait for the Leak. Clean Gutters Stop $4,000 Repairs."
Use as: educational ad / blog headline
Why it works: Reframes a $149 service as cheap insurance against a $4,000 problem. Sells on avoided pain, not features.
"Booked Solid by Word of Mouth — Here's Why"
Use as: brand story headline
Why it works: Curiosity gap ("here's why") + the strongest possible social proof (we don't even need to advertise).
"How to Tell If Your Gutters Are About to Cost You Thousands"
Use as: lead-magnet / video title
Why it works: "How to tell if..." triggers self-diagnosis. People click to check whether they're at risk.
"Same-Day Gutter Cleaning When the Forecast Says Storm"
Use as: urgency ad
Why it works: Ties your service to an external deadline (weather) that creates real, honest urgency.
"15 Years. 4 States. 50,000 Gutters. One Standard."
Use as: about / trust headline
Why it works: Rhythm of escalating numbers builds authority, then "one standard" lands the value: consistency.

Section 2 — Email & Text Subject Lines

For customer follow-ups, seasonal reminders, and re-booking campaigns.

"Your gutters are due (before the leaves drop)"
Seasonal re-book reminder
Why it works: Personal ("your"), specific timing, and a natural deadline. Reads like a helpful nudge, not a sales blast.
"Quick question about your gutters, [name]"
Re-engagement
Why it works: Curiosity + personalization. "Quick question" gets opened because it feels low-effort and personal.
"Storm's coming Thursday — want us out before it?"
Weather-triggered offer
Why it works: Real urgency tied to a real event. Feels like you're looking out for them, not selling.
"It's been about a year since we cleaned yours"
Annual re-book
Why it works: Reminds them of the relationship and the recurring need without any pressure.
"Saved you a spot this week"
Scarcity / assumptive booking
Why it works: Implies demand and does the deciding for them. "Saved you a spot" feels like a favor.
"Re: the repair we flagged last visit"
Upsell follow-up
Why it works: "Re:" feels like an ongoing conversation, not a cold pitch. References something they already know about.

Section 3 — Ad & Social Hooks

First lines that stop the scroll. Use on Facebook, Nextdoor, Instagram, X.

"I've never run a single ad in 15 years. Here's how I stayed booked."
Brand-story hook
Why it works: Pattern interrupt — a business owner admitting they don't advertise makes people lean in. Authenticity sells.
"This $149 job just saved a homeowner $6,000. Here's what we found."
Before/after hook
Why it works: Specific dollar contrast + curiosity. People want to see what was "found."
"3 signs your gutters are about to fail (and what it costs if you wait)"
Listicle hook
Why it works: Numbered list promises a quick, scannable payoff. The parenthetical adds stakes.
"The most expensive home repair nobody sees coming starts in the gutters."
Educational hook
Why it works: Big claim + hidden danger. Makes the invisible problem feel urgent.
"We fired three crews before we got this right. Lesson inside."
Story / authority hook
Why it works: Vulnerability + curiosity gap. Admitting mistakes builds trust faster than bragging.

Section 4 — Booking & Phone Scripts

Word-for-word scripts to close more of the calls and texts you already get.

"Thanks for calling, this is Alex. … Got it — I can have someone out tomorrow morning. It's $149 for a full clean and we'll flag anything that needs repair before it gets worse. Want me to lock that in?"
Inbound call close
Why it works: Names price up front, assumes the booking, and adds value (we'll catch problems). Ends with a yes/no close.
"Happy to give you a quote! Quickest way is a couple photos of your gutters texted to this number — I'll send your exact price in a few minutes."
Text quote request
Why it works: Removes the home-visit friction, gives instant gratification, and keeps the conversation moving.
"We're the 4.9-star, 847-review crew in the area — see you tomorrow between 9 and 11. I'll text when we're 20 minutes out."
Booking confirmation
Why it works: Reinforces social proof at the moment of commitment and sets clear, professional expectations.
"I hear you on price. The cheaper guy might save you $40 today — but we're the highest-rated in the area because we don't leave until it's right. Worth it for your roofline."
Price objection
Why it works: Acknowledges the objection, reframes to value + risk, leans on proof. Never argues.

Section 5 — Review Request Scripts

The exact words that earned 847 reviews. (Full system in the 847-Review Playbook.)

"Glad you're happy with it! The biggest thing that helps a small business like mine is a quick Google review — I'll text you the link right now if that's alright?"
In-person ask
Why it works: Asks at peak happiness, is honest about why, and removes friction by offering to send the link immediately.
"Thanks again, [name]! Here's that review link — takes 20 seconds and means the world to us: [link]"
Text with link
Why it works: Low-effort framing ("20 seconds") + genuine gratitude. Sent immediately while the goodwill is fresh.
"No pressure at all — if you have a sec, that review really helps us out: [link]. Either way, thanks for trusting us with the job."
2-day follow-up nudge
Why it works: Gives an easy out ("no pressure"), which paradoxically increases compliance. Recovers ~1/3 of forgetters.

Section 6 — Review Responses

Replying to every review tells future readers you care. Templates for both kinds.

"Thank you so much, [name]! It was a pleasure getting your gutters cleared before the season. We're always one call away if you need anything."
5-star reply
Why it works: Specific to the job (not generic), warm, and plants the seed for repeat business.
"I'm sorry your experience didn't meet our standard, [name] — that's on us and I want to make it right. Please call me directly at [phone] so I can fix this personally. — Alex, owner"
Negative review reply
Why it works: Calm, accountable, takes it offline. The next reader sees an owner who handles problems like a pro — which sells.
