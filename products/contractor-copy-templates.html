🎯 The Objection Handling Swipe File

The exact words that turned "no" into "yes" across 50,000+ jobs. Every objection you'll hear, and the script that flips it. Tap "Copy" to grab any line and paste it into your own sales conversations.

How to use this: Don't memorize word-for-word. Understand the psychology behind each response — then adapt it to your voice and your trade. Every "Why it works" tells you the principle so you can improvise when a new objection comes up.

Price Objections

The most common objection — and the easiest to flip when you know how.

"That's too expensive" / "The other guy is cheaper"
"I hear you. The other guys might be cheaper — but we're the highest-rated crew in the area for a reason. We don't leave until it's right, and if we find a problem, we flag it before it becomes a $4,000 repair. Is $149 worth knowing your roofline is clear?"
Why it works: Acknowledge without arguing. Reframe price as insurance against bigger cost. End with a question that makes THEM do the math.
"Can you do it for less?" / "Any discounts?"
"I hear you on the price. Here's what I can do — I'll add a free 20-point inspection on top of the clean so you know the full picture. That normally costs $49 extra. You're getting more value, not a lower price."
Why it works: Never discount — ADD value instead. Protects your price, makes them feel like they won, and the "free" inspection is actually a selling tool (it finds upsells).
"I'll just do it myself"
"Totally fair — a lot of our customers started that way. Two things they found: it takes a full Saturday and a 30-foot ladder is the #1 source of homeowner injuries. For $149, you get your weekend back and you don't end up in the ER. Your call."
Why it works: Respect their capability (don't insult them). Then stack the hidden costs: time + risk. End with "your call" — no pressure, but they're now weighing a Saturday and an ER visit vs. $149.

Timing Objections

"Not right now" is the polite version of "no" — unless you know how to reframe urgency.

"I need to think about it"
"Totally fair. What specifically do you want to think through? If it's the timing, I can hold the spot for 24 hours. If it's the price, I can walk through exactly what's included. What's your biggest hesitation?"
Why it works: "I need to think about it" is NEVER the real objection. It's a polite cover for something else — usually price or trust. Find the real objection and address it.
"Not right now" / "Maybe later"
"No problem at all. Quick heads-up though — we're booking about two weeks out right now, and once the fall rush hits, it goes to three or four. If you want a spot before that, I'd grab it now. Otherwise, I'll follow up in a few weeks."
Why it works: Real scarcity, not fake. The seasonal rush is real. Plant the timeline so they understand "later" has a cost — without being pushy.
"I'm getting other quotes first"
"Smart — you should always compare. Here's what I'd ask the other guys: are they insured? Do they have reviews you can check? And do they guarantee the work? We're 4.9 stars across 847 reviews and we carry $2M in insurance. When you're ready, I'll have a spot."
Why it works: You just set the evaluation criteria. They'll now measure every competitor against YOUR standards. And you left the door open without being needy.

Trust Objections

When they don't know you yet — these build credibility fast.

"How do I know you're any good?"
"Fair question. We've got 847 Google reviews at 4.9 stars — you can read them yourself. I've been doing this for 15 years, we carry $2M in insurance, and if you're not happy, you don't pay. That's the deal."
Why it works: Specific numbers (847, 4.9, 15 years, $2M) are more credible than "we're great." And the guarantee removes the last bit of risk.
"I've been burned by contractors before"
"I hear that a lot, and honestly, it's why I built the business the way I did. We text you a confirmation before we arrive. We photo-document everything before and after. We don't leave until you walk it with us and sign off. And if anything goes wrong, my cell is 856-874-6640 — not a call center, not a voicemail. Me."
Why it works: You just addressed every reason they've been burned in the past: no-shows, hidden damage, no accountability. Specificity is credibility.

Closing the Phone Call

These are the final moves — turning "interested" into "booked."

The assumptive close (use on every call)
"I've got tomorrow 9–11 or Thursday 1–3. Which works better for you?"
Why it works: Never ask "would you like to book?" — that's a question they can say no to. Instead, give two options. They're choosing between slots, not between booking and not booking.
The calendar close (when they hesitate)
"No problem — I'll pencil you in for tomorrow at 10 and text you the confirmation. If anything changes, just text me back. No commitment."
Why it works: Now they have to actively CANCEL instead of passively say yes. Cancellation friction is stronger than booking friction.
The social proof close (when they're on the fence)
"We're the 4.9-star, 847-review crew in the area. We just did the house on [nearby street] last week — if you want, I can send you some photos of the work."
Why it works: Social proof + locality. "Your neighbor on [street]" makes it real and immediate. Photos prove it.

Post-Sale Closes

Closing doesn't stop when the job is done. The best revenue comes AFTER the sale.

The review ask (do this on every job)
"Glad you're happy with it! The biggest thing that helps a small business like mine is a quick Google review — I'll text you the link right now if that's alright?"
Why it works: Ask at peak happiness, be honest about why, and remove friction by offering to send the link immediately. 40%+ conversion rate when done right.
The referral close (after a 5-star review)
"Thanks for the review — that means a lot. Quick question: do you know any neighbors or friends who might need their gutters done? If you send them our way, I'll give you $25 off your next clean."
Why it works: The best time to ask for a referral is RIGHT AFTER they've publicly endorsed you. They're already in "promoter" mode. The $25 is a thank-you, not a bribe.
The seasonal re-book (annual outreach)
"Hey [name], it's been about a year since we cleaned your gutters. Fall is coming — want me to get you on the schedule before the rush? Reply C to confirm."
Why it works: Personal, timely, and assumes they want to rebook (not "are you interested?"). "Reply C" makes it one-tap easy.
