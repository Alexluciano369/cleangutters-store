📋Bonus 1 — The Daily Operating ChecklistKit only
The one-page routine that kept four states running:
  • Morning: Confirm today's jobs by text. Check weather. Route by geography (one zip cluster per day).
  • On every job: Photo before → do the work → photo after → walk the customer through it → ask for the review → text the link before leaving the driveway.
  • End of day: Log jobs completed, payments collected, reviews requested. Set aside 25–30% of revenue for taxes immediately.
  • Weekly: Re-book reminders to last year's customers. Respond to every review. Review your numbers (revenue/job, jobs/day, repeat rate).
💰Bonus 2 — The Pricing Calculator FrameworkKit only
Never guess a price again. The formula:
  • Step 1: Add monthly costs (truck, gas, insurance, tools, software, YOUR salary). Divide by billable hours = your true cost/hour.
  • Step 2: Set your base job at 2.5–3× your cost/hour for the time it takes. (Most beginners price at 1× and go broke.)
  • Step 3: Build three tiers — Basic, Clean+Inspect (your anchor), and Full Service. Most customers pick the middle.
  • Step 4: Raise prices 10% a year. You'll lose the cheapest, least-loyal 20% and make more from the rest.
📞Bonus 3 — The 90-Day Launch PlanKit only
A week-by-week sequence to go from zero to a booked calendar:
  • Weeks 1–2: Neighborhood sweep + text everyone you know. Get your first 10 jobs. Set up Google Business Profile.
  • Weeks 3–6: Run the review system on every job. Get to 20+ reviews. Start re-booking happy customers.
  • Weeks 7–10: Dial in pricing tiers. Track your numbers. Test one paid lead source (Google LSA) against your cost-per-job.
  • Weeks 11–13: If you're maxed out, document your process and bring on your first part-time helper. You're now a business, not a job.
